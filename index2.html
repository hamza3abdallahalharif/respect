Respect People

Respecting people's information means recognizing the importance of their privacy and treating their personal details with care and consideration. Here are some simple ways to show respect for others' information:
Keep it confidential: If someone shares personal information with you, don't share it with others without their consent.
Ask before sharing: If you're unsure whether you can share someone else's info, always ask them first.
Be mindful of context: Understand that personal details may be shared for specific purposes, and respect that context when handling the information.
Don't pry: Respect people's boundaries and avoid asking for information that feels too personal or intrusive.
Secure sensitive information: If you're handling sensitive data (like passwords, financial info, or medical details), make sure it's stored or shared securely.
Respect their choices: If someone decides not to share certain information, respect their decision without pressuring them.
